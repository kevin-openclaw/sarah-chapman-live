About

One of the First Female House DJs in the UK

Sarah Chapman is a pivotal figure in UK house music history—a true pioneer whose influence spans from Brighton's underground clubs to international stages, from pirate radio to Kiss FM performances.

"Sarah Chapman is a total inspiration to many, a wonderful person, and a phenomenal DJ who played at some of the most legendary venues in UK house music history."

— Fellow DJs and promoters

Brighton House Pioneer

One of the first female DJs in the UK house music scene, Sarah commanded legendary venues across Brighton and the South Coast throughout the 1990s and beyond. From the Empire in Bognor Regis to Zap Club and Escape Club in Brighton, to residencies spanning Portsmouth and the Isle of Wight—Sarah built an empire of dance floors.

Her "Tonic at the Empire" residency in Bognor Regis became the stuff of legend, while performances at Glasgow's Sub Club and Sterns cemented her reputation as a DJ who could command any room, anywhere.

Radio Legend

Sarah's radio career spans pirate stations to commercial success—performances on Kiss FM showcased her skills to a national audience, while her deep connection to the scene made her a trusted voice for BBC Radio 1.

Featured on Face Magazine covers and BBC Television's New Year Sports Roundup (1996), Sarah's influence extended far beyond the DJ booth—she was a cultural force shaping UK dance music history.

Still Commanding Dance Floors

From the 90s golden era to modern festival stages, Sarah continues to command dance floors with the same precision, skill, and commanding presence that made her a pioneer. Recent appearances at Sterns tent show that decades of experience translate into timeless performances.

The Sarah Chapman Sound

Soulful, high-energy sets full of the joy that defined early house music. Known for her crate-digging flair and warm mixing style, her sets were packed with funky basslines, soulful vocals, and pure house flavour.

Radio

From Pirate to Prime Time

Sarah's radio journey spans from underground pirate stations to major commercial broadcasters.

The Underground Years

Started on pirate radio, bringing underground house music to listeners who couldn't find it anywhere else. Raw, authentic, and fearless broadcasting.

Friends FM • Radioactive • Surf FM

Commercial Success

Graduated to major commercial stations, with regular performances on Kiss FM establishing her reputation nationally. Her insider knowledge of the scene made her a trusted voice for industry professionals and fans alike.

BBC Radio 1 • Kiss FM • Surf 107.2 • Juice 107.2

BBC Recognition

Music featured on BBC Television New Year Sports Roundup (1996). As a trusted voice for BBC Radio 1, Sarah provided weekend scene updates when the legendary Pete Tong needed to know what was happening on the ground.

BBC Television • BBC Radio 1
