About

Pioneering the Scene

One of the first female DJs in the UK house music scene during the 1990s. From Brighton's underground to international stages, from pirate radio to Kiss FM—an unmatched pedigree.

Dance Floor Pioneer

From commanding the Zap Club and Escape Club in Brighton to being Popular in Portsmouth, Bangin' in Bognor, and Doin' Alright on the Isle of Wight—Sarah built an empire across the South Coast. When Pete Tong needed weekend scene intel for BBC Radio 1, he called Sarah. Featured on Face Magazine covers and BBC TV, her pedigree spans decades of dance culture evolution.

Ongoing Legacy

From the 90s golden era to modern festival stages, Sarah continues to command dance floors with the same precision, skill, and commanding presence that made her a pioneer of the Brighton house scene.

Sound

The Sarah Chapman Sound

Soulful, high-energy sets full of the joy that defined early house music. Known for her crate-digging flair and warm mixing style, her sets were packed with funky basslines, soulful vocals, and pure house flavour.

Uplifting House • American Garage • 90s Classics

Radio

From Pirate to Prime Time

Sarah's radio journey spans from underground pirate stations to major commercial broadcasters.

The Underground Years

Started on pirate radio, bringing underground house music to listeners who couldn't find it anywhere else. Raw, authentic, and fearless broadcasting.

Friends FM • Radioactive • Surf FM

Commercial Success

Graduated to major commercial stations. Regular contact for Pete Tong's BBC Radio 1 show—when the legendary DJ needed weekend scene updates, Sarah was who he called.

BBC Radio 1 • Kiss FM • Surf 107.2 • Juice 107.2

BBC Recognition

Music featured on BBC TV New Year Sports Roundup (1996), and Pete Tong's trusted contact for BBC Radio 1 weekend scene reports. When the nation's most influential dance music DJ needed to know what was happening, he called Sarah.
