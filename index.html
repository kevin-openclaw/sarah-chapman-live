About

Pioneering the Scene

Sarah Chapman broke barriers as one of the first female DJs in the UK house music scene during the 1990s. From Brighton's underground to international stages, from pirate radio to Kiss FM, she's built an unmatched pedigree while continuing to DJ worldwide.

Dance Floor Pioneer

From commanding the Zap Club and Escape Club in Brighton to being Popular in Portsmouth, Bangin' in Bognor, and Doin' Alright on the Isle of Wight—Sarah built an empire across the South Coast. When Pete Tong needed weekend scene intel for BBC Radio 1, he called Sarah. Featured on Face Magazine covers and BBC TV, her pedigree spans decades of dance culture evolution.

Dual Expertise

Commercial airline pilot by day, DJ by passion. Sarah proves you can master multiple worlds—commanding both aircraft and dance floors with the same precision, skill, and commanding presence.

Commanding dance floors across the South Coast with iconic residencies: Zap Club & Escape Club (Brighton), Popular in Portsmouth, Bangin' in Bognor, Sizzlin' at Sterns, Blisterin' at BCM, Doin' Alright on the Isle of Wight. Face Magazine cover feature cements status as scene icon.
